# Project Summary

## Project Title

Supplier Performance & Decision Analytics using MCDA, DEA and MOLP

## Context

A fashion accessories retailer wanted to assess 12 suppliers across product quality, customer service, delivery performance and operational efficiency. The business needed a structured approach because supplier performance involved multiple conflicting criteria rather than one clear metric.

## Objective

The objective was to support supplier selection and supplier improvement planning by identifying:

1. strong suppliers to retain,
2. efficient suppliers to benchmark,
3. weak suppliers requiring improvement,
4. practical improvement targets.

## Analytical Workflow

1. **MCDA:** Build Product Quality and Customer Service composite indices.
2. **DEA:** Benchmark supplier efficiency using multiple inputs and outputs.
3. **MOLP / WGP:** Generate improvement targets for inefficient suppliers.
4. **Visualisation:** Summarise rankings, efficiency scores and target gaps.
5. **Business interpretation:** Translate analytical outputs into sourcing recommendations.

## Main Results

Supplier H emerged as the strongest overall supplier. Suppliers A and B also performed strongly and were recommended as key procurement partners. Supplier G required careful interpretation because it had strong operational efficiency but weaker product quality and customer service. Suppliers C, F, J and L were identified as suppliers requiring improvement.

## Recommendation

The retailer should retain H, B and A as core suppliers, use H as the benchmark for development, use G selectively for cost-sensitive orders, and prioritise supplier improvement plans for C, F, J and L.
